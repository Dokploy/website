Community Guides are written and maintained by Dokploy users. They cover real-world setups that fall outside the scope of the official documentation: specific hosting providers, home lab environments, networking tools, and advanced configurations that the community has tested in production.

<Callout type="warn">
  These guides are **not officially supported** by the Dokploy team. Each guide states who contributed it and which Dokploy version it was last tested with. Steps may vary depending on your environment, so always keep a recovery path available before making changes to your servers.
</Callout>

## Why Community Guides?

The official documentation covers what the Dokploy team can test and guarantee across all supported environments. But a lot of valuable knowledge comes from users running Dokploy on specific providers (Hetzner, Proxmox, Raspberry Pi), with specific tools (Cloudflare, Tailscale, WireGuard), or in less common configurations.

Instead of losing that knowledge in closed issues and chat threads, Community Guides give it a permanent, searchable home.

## Contributing a Guide

Have a setup you have tested and want to share? We would love to have it:

1. Fork the [website repository](https://github.com/Dokploy/website)
2. Create your guide in `apps/docs/content/docs/core/community-guides/your-guide-name.mdx`
3. Add your page to the **Community Guides** section in `apps/docs/content/docs/core/meta.json`
4. Open a Pull Request describing what you built and how you verified it

### Guide Template

Start from this template. The `<CommunityGuide />` banner at the top is required — it credits you and tells readers which Dokploy version you tested against:

```mdx
---
title: Your Guide Title
description: One sentence describing what this guide helps the reader accomplish.
---

<CommunityGuide contributor="your-github-username" lastTested="v0.28.6 (May 2026)" />

Brief introduction: what problem does this solve and who is it for?

## Prerequisites

- What the reader needs before starting (accounts, versions, access)

## Setup

### Step 1: ...

Explain each step with the exact commands you ran:

\`\`\`bash
echo "commands the reader can copy"
\`\`\`

## Verification

How does the reader confirm it works?

## Troubleshooting

Common failure modes you hit and how you solved them.
```

### What Makes a Good Guide

- **Tested for real**: you ran it end-to-end on actual servers, not just in theory
- **Exact commands**: copy-pasteable, with placeholders like `<YOUR_SERVER_IP>` clearly marked
- **Failure modes included**: what went wrong for you will go wrong for others
- **Scoped**: one setup per guide — a guide that solves one problem well beats one that solves three halfway
